Try to create a pipeline between blender and python, essentially starting to prepare for the ground for machine learning and training on that in pore calculation.

===
Step 0: Starting Point, Our Biggest Challenge
We begin with just 3 TIF files showing porous materials, but we don't know for sure what their exact porosity values are.

Step 1: Create Ground Truth
We use Blender to turn these TIF files into accurate 3D models and measure their true porosity and pore size distribution(? not sure if its possible). 

Step 2: Verify Our Measurements
We double-check our porosity calculations using multiple different methods to make sure our numbers are correct. 

Step 3: Create More Examples - Blender
Since 3 samples isn't enough:
    - We create artificial samples with known porosity values
    - We make variations of our original samples

Step 4: Build Machine Learning Models - Python
After that, we find a way to convert the mesh into a tensor.
Then, we use our expanded dataset to train two types of AI models:
    - One that can look at a 3D sample and tell you its porosity
    - Another that can create a new 3D sample when you specify what porosity you want

Step 5: Validation
Use some metrics to determine if:
    - They have the correct porosity we asked for
    - Their pore sizes match realistic distributions
    - Manually check by ourself
